What is Stated on the Waiver

By participating, runners acknowledge the inherent risks associated with trail running and agree to participate at their own risk. The waiver will need to be signed and turned into the race directors before the gun goes off. A race waiver states that the participant understands they are taking part in a physical activity that may involve risks such as falls, weather conditions, or injury. It confirms that the runner agrees they are physically able to participate and are responsible for their own health and safety. The waiver releases the race organizers, volunteers, and sponsors from liability for any accidents, injuries, or losses that may occur during the event. It may also give permission for race staff to obtain medical assistance if needed during the race. Finally, it includes the participant’s agreement to follow race rules along with their signature and date.
